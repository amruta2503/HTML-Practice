🥕 Gajar Halwa Recipe (Serves 4)

"Ingredients"

Carrots (Gajar) – 4 cups (grated, about 5–6 medium carrots)

Full-fat milk – 2 cups

Sugar – ½ cup (adjust to taste)

Ghee (clarified butter) – 2 to 3 tablespoons

Cardamom powder – ½ teaspoon

Cashews – 6 to 8 (chopped)

Almonds – 6 to 8 (chopped)

Raisins – 1 tablespoon (optional)

🍳 Instructions:

Step 1: Grate the Carrots

Wash, peel, and grate the carrots using a grater.

Step 2: Cook Carrots in Milk

In a heavy-bottom pan or kadai, add grated carrots and milk.

Cook on medium heat. Keep stirring until the milk reduces and thickens (about 15–20 minutes).

Step 3: Add Sugar and Stir

Add sugar and stir. The mixture will become slightly watery—cook it until the water dries up.

Step 4: Add Ghee

Now add ghee and cook for 5–10 more minutes until halwa turns glossy and thick.

Step 5: Flavor with Cardamom

Add cardamom powder and mix well.

Step 6: Fry and Add Dry Fruits

In a small pan, heat a bit of ghee, fry cashews, almonds, and raisins, and mix them into the halwa.

📝 Tips:

For richer taste, use khoya (mawa) or condensed milk instead of part of the milk.

You can also pressure cook carrots with milk to save time.

Best served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream!
